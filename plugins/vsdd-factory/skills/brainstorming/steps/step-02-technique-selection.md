# Step 2: Technique Selection

> **Parent skill:** `brainstorming` — see `../SKILL.md` for the full workflow.
> **Agent:** product-owner
> **This step:** recommend two or three techniques fitted to the session frame, and let your human partner choose.

## Inputs

- Session frame from `step-01-session-setup.md`
- The technique table in SKILL.md § Step 2

## Outputs

- One or two selected techniques with running order
- Human's confirmation that the selection feels right

## Procedure

1. Map the session frame onto the technique table from SKILL.md:

   | If frame says... | Recommend |
   |---|---|
   | Greenfield, low specificity | Brain dump → Mind mapping |
   | Greenfield, audience clear | Mind mapping → Constraint removal |
   | Improvement, existing product | SCAMPER → Reverse brainstorming |
   | "We can't do X" friction | Constraint removal → SCAMPER |
   | Multiple stakeholders, conflict | Six thinking hats |
   | Hidden assumptions suspected | Reverse brainstorming |

2. Present two or three recommendations, not the whole table:

   > "Based on what you told me, I'd suggest one of these:
   > **Option A: [technique].** Best when you want to [goal]. Roughly [time].
   > **Option B: [technique].** Best when you want to [goal]. Roughly [time].
   > Want to pick, or want me to choose?"

3. For each recommended technique, give a one-sentence "what it feels like":
   - **Brain dump:** "You talk, I capture. No structure until we're done."
   - **SCAMPER:** "I walk you through seven prompts: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse."
   - **Reverse brainstorming:** "We try to make the idea fail on purpose, then invert."
   - **Mind mapping:** "We pick a center, branch outward, then branch the branches."
   - **Constraint removal:** "I name a constraint, you imagine it's gone, you build."
   - **Six thinking hats:** "We rotate through six perspectives — facts, feelings, risks, benefits, creativity, process."

4. If your human partner cannot decide, pick for them with a one-line rationale ("Going with SCAMPER — you have an existing product and the friction is in feature design") and proceed.

5. Confirm running order and rough timebox. Default: 20–30 minutes per technique, two techniques max per session.

## Decision points

- **Two techniques or one?** Default to one. Add a second only if the first reveals a specific gap.
- **Switching mid-session** is allowed and expected if the chosen technique stalls. Step 3 covers that.

## Failure modes

- **Recommending all six techniques.** Paralysis. Pick two or three, defend them, move on.
- **Picking the technique you find interesting instead of the one that fits.** The frame drives the choice.
- **Skipping the "what it feels like" descriptions.** A human who does not understand the technique will disengage two minutes in.

## Quality gate

- [ ] At least one technique selected
- [ ] Human understands what the technique will feel like
- [ ] Rough timebox agreed
- [ ] If two techniques selected, the order is explicit

## Hand-off to next step

Pass selected technique(s) and order to `step-03-facilitated-ideation.md`.
