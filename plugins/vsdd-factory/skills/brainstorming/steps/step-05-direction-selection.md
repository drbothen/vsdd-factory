# Step 5: Direction Selection

> **Parent skill:** `brainstorming` — see `../SKILL.md` for the full workflow.
> **Agent:** orchestrator
> **This step:** help your human partner choose exactly one direction to carry forward into a brief.

## Inputs

- The 2–3 strongest concepts from `step-04-synthesis.md`
- The tensions list from step 4
- Session frame (constraints matter heavily here)

## Outputs

- One selected direction with explicit rationale
- A "parking lot" of unselected concepts they want to remember for later

## Procedure

1. Frame the choice clearly:
   > "We have [N] concepts on the table. The brief that comes out of this session covers exactly one of them — others can be briefed separately later. Let's pick one."

2. Present concepts side by side. Use a comparison table:

   | | Concept A | Concept B | Concept C |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | Problem | ... | ... | ... |
   | Audience | ... | ... | ... |
   | Differentiator | ... | ... | ... |
   | Effort to v1 | low/med/high | ... | ... |
   | Risk | ... | ... | ... |

   Effort and risk are your judgment. Be honest, not flattering.

3. Ask: "Which one pulls you?" Wait. Let the silence sit.

4. **If they pick clearly:** ask "why?" and capture the rationale verbatim. The rationale matters more than the choice — it tells the brief which framing to use.

5. **If they cannot decide:** offer the feasibility-vs-impact matrix.
   > "Plot each concept on two axes — how feasible is it given your constraints, how big is the impact if it works. The top-right wins."

   Walk through the plot together. If still stuck, recommend one with a one-line rationale tied to step 1: "I'd pick [X] because [reason from constraints]."

6. **If they reject all concepts:** capture the rejection reasons verbatim — they reveal the real criteria. Either return to step 3 with a different technique, or end the session and reframe later.

7. Once chosen, ask the parking-lot question:
   > "The other concepts — note them in the report so you can come back later, or are they truly off the table?"

   Parked concepts go in the report under "Not chosen — reference for later."

8. Confirm one more time before moving to step 6:
   > "Direction is [X]. The brief I write next will be about [X], not the others. Confirmed?"

## Decision points

- **Pick one, brief one.** Non-negotiable. Multi-concept briefs become incoherent products.
- **Combining two concepts** → only allow if they share the same audience and the same problem. Otherwise it is two products in a trench coat.
- **Feasibility-vs-impact matrix is the tiebreaker**, not the default.

## Failure modes

- **Letting the human "keep options open."** That is the failure mode this step exists to prevent. Push for one.
- **Picking the most ambitious concept because it sounds exciting.** Re-read the constraints from step 1 out loud if they reach past them.
- **Recommending without rationale tied to step 1.** "I just like it" is not a rationale.

## Quality gate

- [ ] Exactly one direction selected
- [ ] Selection rationale captured verbatim
- [ ] Parking-lot decision made for unselected concepts
- [ ] Human explicitly confirmed the chosen direction

## Hand-off to next step

Pass the selected direction, rationale, parked concepts, and rejected ideas to `step-06-write-report.md`.
