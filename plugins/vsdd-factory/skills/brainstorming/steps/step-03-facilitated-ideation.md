# Step 3: Facilitated Ideation

> **Parent skill:** `brainstorming` — see `../SKILL.md` for the full workflow.
> **Agent:** product-owner
> **This step:** run the selected technique(s), draw ideas out of your human partner, capture everything.

## Inputs

- Selected technique(s) and order from `step-02-technique-selection.md`
- Session frame from step 1

## Outputs

- A raw idea log: every idea generated, in order, with the prompt that surfaced it
- Notes on which prompts produced energy and which fell flat

## Procedure

1. State the technique you are starting with and the timebox. Then start.

2. Use the technique-specific scripts below. Stay in the technique — do not collapse into generic "what else?" prompting.

### Brain dump

> "Tell me everything that's in your head about this. Don't filter, don't organize, don't worry if it's a good idea. I'll capture and we'll sort later. Go."

Stay quiet. Only interject with: "Keep going" / "What else?" / "Anything from the edges of your thinking?" After they slow twice, ask one final time, then stop.

### SCAMPER

Walk through all seven prompts. Use this exact framing:

- **Substitute:** "What part of [thing] could you swap out — material, process, person, technology?"
- **Combine:** "What could you merge with [thing]? Another product, workflow, team's job?"
- **Adapt:** "Where else has someone solved a problem like this? What could you borrow from a different industry?"
- **Modify:** "What if you made [thing] much bigger? Much smaller? What if you exaggerated one feature?"
- **Put to other use:** "Who else could use [thing]? What if you sold it to a totally different audience?"
- **Eliminate:** "What could you remove and still have something valuable? What's the smallest version that works?"
- **Reverse:** "What if you did it backward? What if the user did the work the system does today, or vice versa?"

Capture each response before moving on. Do not skip prompts even if your partner pushes back — sometimes the seventh breaks loose.

### Reverse brainstorming

> "We're going to try to make this fail on purpose. How would you guarantee that [thing] is a disaster?"

After 8–12 failure modes, invert: "Now flip each one. What does the opposite look like?"

### Mind mapping

> "Center node: [topic]. Give me five branches off of it — categories, aspects, dimensions."

Capture as an indented list. Pick the branch with most energy, ask: "Five sub-branches off [branch]." Recurse twice.

### Constraint removal

> "Tell me a constraint you're stuck behind. Budget, time, tech, politics, physics — pick one."

Then: "It's gone. The constraint does not exist. What do you build now?" After they answer: "Now put 50% of the constraint back. What survives?"

### Six thinking hats

Rotate through, one at a time:
- **White (facts):** "What do we actually know about this? No opinions."
- **Red (feelings):** "How do you feel about it? Gut reaction, no justification."
- **Black (caution):** "What are the risks? What could go wrong?"
- **Yellow (benefits):** "Best case — what's the upside?"
- **Green (creativity):** "Wild ideas. Things you'd be embarrassed to suggest in a meeting."
- **Blue (process):** "What perspective haven't we taken?"

3. **Facilitation behaviors throughout (from SKILL.md § Step 3):**
   - Probing: "What problem does this solve? Who cares about this?"
   - Challenging: "Why does it have to work that way?"
   - Connecting: "That's related to what you said earlier about X — have you considered..."
   - Capturing dismissed ideas: "Noting that one even though you said it's bad — sometimes those have kernels."

4. When the technique is exhausted (energy drops, partner repeats, or timebox hits), stop and ask: "Want to switch techniques, take a break, or move to synthesis?"

## Decision points

- **Stalling within 5 minutes** → switch. Monosyllabic answers? Switch. "I don't know" three times? Switch.
- **One technique produced enough material** → do not run a second for completeness. Move to synthesis.
- **Human starts synthesizing on their own** → let them. Capture but don't drag back.

## Failure modes

- **Generating ideas yourself instead of drawing them out.** You facilitate. If you catch yourself proposing concepts, stop and ask a question.
- **Filtering during capture.** Capture everything. Synthesis is step 4, not now.
- **Letting your partner spiral on one idea for 20 minutes.** Acknowledge, capture, redirect: "Good — let me come back to that. For now, what else?"

## Quality gate

- [ ] At least one technique fully executed (not abandoned mid-prompt)
- [ ] At least 8–10 distinct ideas captured (lower bar for narrow domains)
- [ ] Raw idea log preserved verbatim, not pre-filtered
- [ ] Human signaled they are ready for synthesis
